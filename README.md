# 🛠 Camp Bowman Maker Space

## 🌲 Overview
Welcome to the **Camp Bowman Maker Space**! 

Our program is designed to bridge the gap between traditional scouting skills and modern technology. While our handicraft area continues to provide hands-on experience with traditional tools (woodworking, leatherwork, etc.), the Maker Space introduces **Digital Fabrication** to our camp experience.

## 🎯 Current Pilot: Custom Nametags/Badges
We are currently piloting our laser cutter program with the production of custom nametags. This project allows Scouts to explore the intersection of design and craftsmanship.

### 🛠 Capabilities & Limitations
To ensure the safety of our Scouts and the longevity of our equipment, we have strict guidelines on material usage:

*   ✅ **ALLOWED MATERIALS:** Wood and Leather only.
*   ❌ **PROHIBITED MATERIALS:** No plastics, acrylics, paper, or any other materials are to be used at this time.
*   **Supervision Required:** Supervision by staff approved individuals is required for all operation. Any makerspace tool requires direct supervision for the duration of the job run.

## 📂 How to Use This Repository

This repository is organized to support two different types of makers:

### 1. The "Quick Maker" (Using Templates)
If you want to experience the magic of laser cutting without the complexity of graphic design, we have provided ready-to-go templates.
*   **Where to find them:** Navigate to the `/laser` directory.
*   **How to use:** Download the `.lbrn2` (LightBurn) files. These are pre-configured for our machine. You can open them in LightBurn and simply change the text to your name before cutting.

### 2. The "Designer" (Custom Projects)
If you want to create your own unique badge or design, you are encouraged to create your own vector files.
*   **Software:** We recommend using vector-based software (like Inkscape, Illustrator).
*   **Workflow:**
**   Ensure you have the dimensions and material that you will be cutting, If unsure check with the handicraft staff. Keep in mind new materials may require experimentation to properly cut/etch. Extra material may be required.
**   Design your shape
**   export it as an `.svg`
**   Work with the handicraft staff bring to load your design into LightBurn and cut
